  • Student Nonviolent Coordinating Comittee v. Alabama (1964)
    Case
    1/9
    Detail:

    A case involving the right of students to access voter registration records in Alabama.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court ruled in favor of the students, affirming their right to access public records related to voter registration.

  • Lopez v. Candaele (1998)
    Case
    2/9
    Detail:

    A student claimed that his free speech rights were violated when he was prohibited from distributing religious pamphlets at his college.

    Conclusion:

    The Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals ruled in favor of the student, stating that the college's policies restricting distribution were unconstitutional.

  • Safford Unified School District v. Redding (2009)
    Case
    3/9
    Detail:

    A student was strip-searched by school officials based on suspicion of possessing prescription-strength ibuprofen.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court ruled in favor of the student, stating that the strip search violated her Fourth Amendement rights.

  • Smith v. Board of School Commissioners of Mobile County (1979)
    Case
    4/9
    Detail:

    A case addressing racial discrimination in hiring practices in a school system in Alabama.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court rule against the school system, finding that its hiring practices were racially discriminatory and violated the Title VII of the Civil Rights Act.

  • Keyishian v. Board of Regents (1967)
    Case
    5/9
    Detail:

    A case involving New York's loyalty oath requirements for teachers and professors at public universities.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court struck down the loyalty oath requirements, safeguarding academic freedom and free speech rights for educators.

  • West Viringa State Board of Education v. Barnette (1943)
    Case
    6/9
    Detail:

    A case dealing with First Admendment rights and whether students could be complelled to salute the flad and recite the Pledge of Allegiance.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court ruled in favor of the students, stating that such compulsion violated their First Amendment rights.

  • Plyler v. Doe (1982)
    Case
    7/9
    Detail:

    A case addressing the rights of undocumented immigrant children to receive a public education.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court ruled that denying education to undocumented children violated the Equal Protection Clause and allowed them access to public education.

  • Epperson v. Arkansas (1968)
    Case
    8/9
    Detail:

    A case challenging a state law that prohibited the teaching of evolution in public schools.

    Conclusion:

    The Supreme Court ruled in favor of the teacher, declaring the law unconstitutional and affirming the importance of teaching evolution.

  • Hazelwood School District v. Kuhlmeier (1988)
    Case
    9/9
    Detail:

    The Supreme Court ruled that a school's policy allowing censorship of articles in a student newspaper violated student's First Amendment rights.

    Conclusion:

    The school policy permitting prior restraint on student journalism was overruled.
